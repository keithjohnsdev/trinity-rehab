SPEECH THERAPY

Our Speech and Language pathologists successfully treat patients using an extensive set of skills and techniques that address:
  • Articulation and phonology (formation and production of sounds)
  • Apraxia (motor speech disorder)
  • Fluency (stuttering, blocking and repetitions)
  • Voice: monitoring appropriate voice quality, pitch and loudness
  • Receptive language (comprehension)
  • Expressive language in oral, written, graphic and manual modalities
  • Social communication: Pragmatics (understanding and using language in social situations)
  • Cognitive- Communication (attention, memory, executive functions)
  • Augmentative and alternative Communication (ACC support)
  • Swallowing and other aerodigestive functions
  • Sensory awareness related to communication and swallowing

Who May Benefit from Speech-Language Therapy?

Speech-Language Therapy can be beneficial for people with a variety of diagnoses including but not limited to:
  • Autism Related Disorders
  • Down Syndrome and other genetic disorders
  • Cerebral Palsy and other neurological diagnoses
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Apraxia of Speech
  • Difficulty in Receptive-Expressive Language
  • Hearing Impairment
  • Auditory Processing and Discrimination
  • Oral Motor Weakness
  • Dysphagia (swallowing disorder)
