OCCUPATIONAL THERAPY

Occupational Therapists apply specific techniques to address difficulties with:
  • Daily Living/Self Care Skills including feeding, dressing, and grooming
  • Hand coordination skills required for activities such as cutting with scissors, writing, coloring, etc
  • Fine Motor Control and Organization
  • Bilateral hand skills
  • Visual perceptual skills
  • Attention
  • Following Directions
  • Sensory Integration
  • Sensory Processing such as Organization and Self-Regulation

Who May Benefit from OT?

Occupational Therapy treats a variety of diagnoses that affect mobility, including but not limited to:
  • Deficits in fine motor and visual motor skills affecting activities of daily living
  • Autism Related Disorders
  • Sensory/Motor Disorders
  • ADD/ADHD
  • Developmental Disabilities
  • Down Syndrome and other genetic diagnoses
  • Cerebral Palsy and other neurological diagnoses
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Sensory Integration Disorders
