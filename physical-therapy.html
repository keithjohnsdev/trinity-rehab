PHYSICAL THERAPY

Our Physical Therapists treat patients using an extensive set of skills and techniques that include:
  • Manual therapy techniques
  • Range of Motion
  • Therapeutic exercises and activities
  • Activities of Daily Living (ADL) training and adaption to client’s home setting
  • Massage/Myofascial Release
  • Gait training
  • Motor planning
  • Vestibular Activities (balance)
  • Proprioceptive training
  • Strength training
  • Positioning handling
  • Stretching
  • Postural Training
  • Sitting/Standing balance
  • Sensory Stimulation
  • Transfer training
  • Recommendations for assistive devices
  • Evaluation for equipment needs
  • Proprioceptive training

Who May Benefit from PT?

Physical Therapy treats a variety of diagnoses that affect mobility, including but not limited to:
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Genetic Disorders
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Musculoskeletal Disorders
  • Spina Bifida
  • Neuromuscular Disorders
  • Down syndrome
  • Severe autism
  • Musculoskeletal injuries
  • Orthopedic and post-surgical conditions
  • Intellectual Disabilities
